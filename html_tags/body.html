Mahatma Gandhi

About Mahatma Gandhi

Full Name: Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi Born: October 2, 1869, Porbandar, Gujarat, India Died: January 30, 1948, New Delhi, India Title: Mahatma (meaning "Great Soul"), Bapu (Father) Famous For: Leader of Indian independence movement, Non-violent resistance (Satyagraha)

Brief Summary:

Mahatma Gandhi was a key leader in India’s struggle for independence from British rule. He is best known for using non-violent civil disobedience to challenge injustice and colonial laws. His approach inspired freedom movements and civil rights campaigns across the world.Gandhi was born into a Hindu family in Porbandar, Gujarat.He studied law in London and later worked in South Africa, where he first experienced racial discrimination.His experiences in South Africa shaped his views on justice and resistance.On January 30, 1948, Gandhi was assassinated by Nathuram Godse, a Hindu extremist who opposed Gandhi’s views on Hindu-Muslim unity.
